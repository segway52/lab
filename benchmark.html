Path to #1: gap-to-best-in-class action plan

NatWest leads credit cards but trails Santander by 1 point on loans. Each card shows the pillar leader, NatWest's gap, and the activities (with owning team) to close it.

Loans: Organic SoV — gap −20 directional

Leader: Santander (65) · NatWest 45

Santander's loans page was the only bank page ranking directly in sampled "best personal loan rates UK" results. Everything else routes through aggregators (MSE, MoneySuperMarket, Which?, CompareTheMarket). This is the single biggest gap keeping NatWest at #2 on loans.

SEO + Content
Build "best/compare"-intent loan content (rates tables, "loans for X" hubs) that can rank against aggregator listicles; target featured snippets.
Partnerships / Media
Audit aggregator placement and feed accuracy on MSE/MSM — most loan demand routes through them.
SEO Ops
Commission proper SoV tracking (Semrush/Sistrix, ~200-term acquisition basket) to replace this directional sample.

Loans: Experience — gap −7

Leader: Santander (85) · NatWest 78

Santander publishes a full server-rendered rate table by amount band, per-product eligibility checkers and a step-by-step application timeline ("decision within 5 minutes"). TSB (84) is open to non-customers with a 2-minute quote and same-day money. NatWest's QuickQuote is competitive but under-promoted and the hub lacks an inline rate table.

Digital Product / CX
Add an inline rate-by-amount table and application timeline to the loans hub; promote QuickQuote above the fold.
Analytics
Benchmark quote-to-application conversion vs TSB's "2-minute quote, same-day money" proposition.

Cards: Experience — gap −6

Leader: HSBC (86) · NatWest 80

HSBC answers on-page: inline FAQ accordions, an eligibility checker that estimates your credit limit, and per-card rep examples. NatWest links out to FAQs rather than answering on the hub.

Digital Product / CX
Add inline FAQ accordions and a comparison/filter module to the credit-cards hub (HSBC/Barclaycard pattern).
Digital Product
Match HSBC's "estimated credit limit" output in the eligibility checker.

SEO foundations — gap −6 (both products)

Leader: Lloyds (88) · NatWest 82/80

NatWest titles/descriptions are strong, but canonicals are relative URLs, and social-card coverage is thinner than Lloyds, which also runs structured FAQ entities (Yext).

SEO / Web Platform
Fix relative canonicals; complete OpenGraph + Twitter cards; add FAQPage + Product structured data to hub and product pages.
SEO
Add Lighthouse/CWV audits per template — page speed was out of scope for this snapshot.

AI readiness & LLM visibility — NatWest leads

NatWest 88 / 85 · nearest: Santander 78 / 75–78

Only brand with llms.txt; products cited inside AI-generated answers (Longer Balance Transfer card). Contrast: Barclays' loan rates render as "0 APR … £0" and Nationwide's as "Loading…" without JavaScript — invisible to AI crawlers.

SEO / AI Search
Protect the lead: add explicit AI-crawler allows in robots.txt (Santander pattern: GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, CCBot).
Content Ops
Keep llms.txt on a release cadence; extend to rates and eligibility pages.
Insights
Stand up LLM answer tracking (Profound/Otterly or monthly prompt panels across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, AI Overviews) to monitor citation share.

Site health — joint leader (85)

Tied with Santander (85)

Clean robots.txt with sitemaps declared. Santander's robots is the most actively managed in the set (parameter hygiene, crawler tiers, SEO-tool blocking) — worth mirroring. Barclays' robots.txt returns empty; Nationwide timed out once during testing.

SEO Ops
Add parameter-handling rules and quarterly log-file analysis; monitor competitor robots/llms policies quarterly.

Methodology & weights

SEO foundations · 20%Measured from live page source: title/meta quality, canonicals, OpenGraph/Twitter cards, indexability directives, sitemap declaration.
Organic SoV · 15%Directional. Sampled live search results for high-intent queries (best credit cards, balance transfer, best loan rates). Direct rankings score highest; citations within answer content score mid. Harden with a Semrush/Sistrix keyword basket.
AI readiness · 15%Measured: llms.txt presence, robots.txt AI-crawler policy, server-side rendering of rates/rep-examples, inline FAQ extractability.
LLM visibility · 15%Directional. Observed citations of brand products inside AI-generated answer summaries, combined with measured access/extractability signals. Harden with LLM answer-tracking tooling.
Site health · 10%Measured: robots.txt quality, sitemap coverage, redirect/canonical cleanliness, response reliability. Core Web Vitals out of scope (needs Lighthouse/CrUX).
Experience · 25%Measured from page content: eligibility checkers (soft-search), calculators, rate transparency, FAQ depth, application friction (incl. account-gating), product range, supporting guides.

Limitations: point-in-time snapshot (10 Jun 2026) of public hub pages; logged-in journeys, mobile apps, Core Web Vitals, backlink authority and JSON-LD validation were not measurable with this method — add via Lighthouse/CrUX, Semrush/Ahrefs and schema validation before final investment sizing. SoV and LLM-visibility pillars are directional samples and weighted accordingly. Revolut should be re-benchmarked the week its UK credit cards and loans go live.
